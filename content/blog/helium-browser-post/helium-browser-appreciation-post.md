I have been using Helium for almost 8 months and I am loving it. I was a long-time Edge user but after the slopification started I went looking for a better browser.

First I tried Zen, it was really good but the performance isn't the same and it's especially noticeable on older hardware. I didn't have fun with Brave and Vivaldi either, too much slop, useless pushed features. No, I don't want a calendar, contacts, RSS feed, crypto wallet, or Copilot in my browser, what the hell.

Then I found Helium. Pre-configured with ad block and community filters is such a welcome feature. No fingerprinting option, minimalist, no bloat. I love compact mode, and if I have to deal with too many tabs I switch to vertical tabs then go back to compact mode. Bangs for quickly navigating and searching different sites are great too. I love minimalist software and Helium excels at that, it's minimal but definitely not lacking in features. It has plenty, just the ones that actually matter for browsing, not useless crap like other browsers push.

Being in alpha, issues are expected, and there are a couple I ran into while using it. Sometimes sites trigger Cloudflare bot checks too often, I even couldn't enter some sites when it happened a lot, though this was mostly in the early days and hasn't been a problem for about 3 months. More recently I had a gray screen appear after minimizing and restoring the window, but it's been rare and it's infinitely better than dealing with bloated slop browsers.

For the wishlist I'd love an official Fedora DNF package, Windows auto-updates and an auto-hide toolbar option.

Also a question, is there a way to completely remove the left and right borders? I really like the minimal setup and couldn't find an option for it.

Thanks for this great software.
